# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

Cardiff is committed to a respectful, safe, and professional collaboration environment.
We want this project to be welcoming to contributors of different backgrounds, skill levels, identities, and viewpoints.

## Expected Behavior

Participants in this project are expected to:

- communicate clearly and respectfully
- give technical feedback in good faith
- focus criticism on ideas, code, documentation, and outcomes
- respect personal boundaries, privacy, and consent
- credit the work of others accurately
- help keep discussions constructive and useful

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behavior is not acceptable in project spaces:

- harassment, hate speech, or discriminatory conduct
- intimidation, threats, or personal attacks
- sexualized language or unwelcome sexual attention
- doxxing or sharing private information without consent
- plagiarism, misrepresentation, or intentional deception
- retaliation against people who raise concerns in good faith
- repeated disruption of discussions, reviews, or collaboration channels

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, documentation, and any other spaces where Cardiff is represented as a project community.

## Reporting

To report a concern, contact the maintainer at `zecalifornia@up.edu.ph`.
Include links, screenshots, logs, or other context when possible.
Reports will be handled as confidentially as practical.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may remove content, issue warnings, restrict participation, or ban contributors when necessary to protect the community and the project.
Enforcement decisions should be proportional to the behavior and prioritize community safety.
